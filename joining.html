Position Announcement:
Funded PhD Student Researcher

Posted: March 10, 2023 | Status: Active

Position Description

This position is a funded PhD graduate research assistantship under the supervision of Professor Joseph P. Vantassel in the geotechnical engineering program at Virginia Tech. The position is scheduled to start in the fall of 2023. The position will involve applying state-of-the-art subsurface imaging techniques, in particular full waveform inversion, to near-surface geotechnical site characterization. The position will involve field-based data acquisition, data processing and interpretation, and publication of results. The position will require software development and use of high performance computing. The position may involve substantial utilization of machine & deep learning. The ideal candidate will have strong communication skills (written and oral) and a proven ability to deliver on projects. Candidates who may not have all of the technical skills aforementioned and below, but who are willing and interested to learn are especially encouraged to reply to the posting using the link below.

Required Qualifications

  • MS degree in geotechnical engineering or related field.
  • Ability to pursue PhD in civil engineering (geotechnical focus) at Virginia Tech in the fall semester of 2023.
  • Prior experience in geotechnical engineering, near-surface geophysics, or other related field.
  • Prior programming experience (any language).
  • Ability to perform field-based research.

Preferred Qualifications

  • Prior experience with software development (Python or C++ preferred).
  • Knowledge of high performance computing concepts.
  • Familiarity with inverse problems.

Timeline

Review of candidates will begin immediately and end on March 31st.

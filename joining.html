Position Announcement:
Funded PhD Student Researcher

Position Description

This position is a funded PhD graduate research assistantship under the supervision of Professor Joseph P. Vantassel in the geotechnical engineering program at Virginia Tech. The position is scheduled to start in the spring of 2023. The position will involve applying state-of-the-art subsurface imaging techniques (in particular surface wave methods and full waveform inversion) to near-surface geotechnical site characterization. The position will involve field-based data acquisition, data processing and interpretation, and publication of results. The position may require open-source software development and use of high performance computing and machine learning. The ideal candidate will have strong communication skills (written and oral) and a proven ability to deliver on projects. Candidates who may not have all of the technical skills aforementioned and below, but who are willing and interested to learn are especially encouraged to apply.

Required Qualifications

  • MS degree in geotechnical engineering or related field.
  • Ability to pursue PhD in civil engineering (geotechnical focus) at Virginia Tech starting in the spring semester of 2023.
  • Prior experience in geotechnical engineering, near-surface geophysics, or other related field.
  • Prior programming experience (any language).
  • Ability and desire to perform field-based research.

Preferred Qualifications

  • Prior experience with science- or engineering-based software development (Python or C++ preferred).
  • Knowledge of fundamental data science concepts and prior experience with machine learning.
  • Knowledge of high performance computing concepts.

Review of candidates will begin immediately and end on June 15th.
